What is a capybara

Capybaras have heavy, barrel-shaped bodies and short heads, and they have reddish-brown fur on its upper body which passing to yellowish-brown below. Adult capybaras reach up to 130 cm in length and up to 65 kg in weight. There is information about a specimen with weight 105.4 kg. Capybaras have slightly webbed feet, no tail and they have 20 teeth. Their hind legs are slightly longer than their front legs. The muzzles they are aligned with their eyes, nostrils and ears on top of their heads. Females are slightly lighter than males. Females weigh average from 34 to 61 kg, while males usually weigh from 36 to 66 kg.

Distribution

The capybara is found on the shores of various reservoirs in the tropics and temperate parts of Central and South America, east of the Andes - from Panama to Uruguay and to the northeastern parts of Argentina (up to 38°17' Yu. sh., province of Buenos Aires).
